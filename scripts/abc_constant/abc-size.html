Triples of small size

At the Mathematical Institute in Leiden there have been several activities around the ABC conjecture and enumerating ABC-triples of small size, starting with a meeting in September 2005 of the Intercity Number Theory Seminar. In 2007 we started a science promotion project in the Netherlands called Reken mee met ABC, which is also the subject of a paper paper in Dutch [PDF, March 2007] that I wrote with Gillien Geuze.

ABC@Home

One part of the project was a volunteer network called ABC@home [wikipedia] devoted to a distributed computational project that would come up with a complete list of all ABC triples consisting of integers of at most 18 digits. We reached this goal in 2011 with a list of 14482065 triples. The triples are available in the file abctriples_below_1018.gz (gzipped text file, 179 MB). For each triple (a, b, c) with c below 1018 the file has a line with the numbers c and a.

See chapter 7 of the PhD thesis of WIllem Jan Palenstijn for a description of the algorithm used and why we believe that this list is complete. The chapter also contains some interesting observations about the list of triples.

The bachelor thesis of Thijs van Dijk from 2011 addresses the implementation.

In the years 2012-2015 ABC@home has been finding additional triples (a,b,c) with with c in the range 1018 ≤ c < 263. This produced 9345651 additional triples that are available in the file big_triples.gz (gzipped text file, 133 MB).

At this moment ABC@home is no longer active.
